      Tamil temples stand as the birthplace of many traditional art forms. Dances like Bharatanatyam, Koothu, Karagattam, Mayilattam, Poikkaal Kuthirai Aattam, Oyilattam, Devarattam, Bommalattam, Thappattam, Kavadi Aattam and Villupattu originated and flourished here. Devadasis performed daily dances as offerings to the deity. Temples served as prime venues for artistic debut performances. Dance programs were organized during temple festivals. The mandapams provided excellent settings for music and dance. Temples were hubs of community culture. Devotion was expressed through art. Music, dance and rama all emerged from one sacred space. Temples became holy grounds where arts evolved. Even today, traditional dance festivals are held in some temples. People visit temples to experience cultural depth. Tamil heritage excelled in both music and dance. Temples offered a platform for emerging artists. They are living testaments to our rich cultural legacy.
